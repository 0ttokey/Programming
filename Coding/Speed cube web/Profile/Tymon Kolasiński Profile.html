Profile


Tymon Kolasiński

Tymon Kolasiński is a Polish Rubik's Cube speedsolver who is currently tied 2nd place for the world record average of five 3x3x3 solves (by WCA standards), 4.86 seconds, set on the 30th of July 2022.Kolasiński holds the Polish national record in the 3x3x3, 4x4x4, 5x5x5, 6x6x6, and 7x7x7 events, which is also top ten in the world. Before rising to spotlight in cubing for his efficient solutions in NxNxN cubes, he specialized in the Pyraminx, where he previously achieved the world record single and has held the Pyraminx average world record a total of four times. He became the 2019 Pyraminx world champion after winning the event in the WCA World Championship 2019, with a final average of 2.40 seconds. He is also the 2022 European champion of five events. He is best known for his efficient CFOP solutions, utilizing advanced techniques paired with quick thinking in order to stay consistent. As of 16 April 2023, he has won 228 total events in World Cube Association competitions.
